1. Program Overview

NextPhD Bangladesh is a structured mentorship program designed to help Bangladeshi students navigate the US, Canadian, and Australian PhD application process. Over the next 5 months (July – November 2026), we will guide you through every step — from identifying target universities and professors to submitting polished applications.

What You Get

  • Dedicated online dashboard to track your entire application journey
  • Weekly/bi-weekly live sessions with mentors and peers
  • Curated database of 428 PhD openings from 185 universities (US/Canada/Australia)
  • University pool with CS Rankings data for informed decision-making
  • Professor database for targeted outreach
  • Research lab tracker (labs currently hiring PhD students)
  • SOP/CV/writing sample review with mentor feedback
  • Interview preparation resources and mock sessions
  • Peer community via Discord and platform features
Dashboard Access: https://nextphd.elitelab.ai
Login with the credentials sent to your email. Change your password after first login using the button in the top-right corner.

2. Five-Month Roadmap

Each month has clear goals and deliverables. Your progress is tracked on the dashboard.

Month 1 — July 2026: Foundation & Research
Build your profile, identify research interests, and start exploring universities and professors.
  • Complete your Dashboard Profile (target: 100% completeness)
  • Upload your latest CV to the Files section
  • Browse the PhD Openings database (428 openings) — save/bookmark interesting ones
  • Explore the University Pool — shortlist 15–20 universities
  • Start browsing the Professor Pool — identify potential advisors
  • Browse Research Labs section — find labs aligned with your interests
  • Complete the Document Checklist — identify what's missing
  • Payment Due: July 1, 2026
Month 2 — August 2026: Shortlisting & Outreach
Narrow down your target list and begin professor outreach.
  • Finalize your university shortlist (8–12 universities) in University Tracker
  • Research 20–30 professors whose work aligns with your interests
  • Add target professors to your Professor Tracker with outreach status
  • Draft your first cold emails using the Cold Email Template Generator
  • Begin sending outreach emails to professors (track status on dashboard)
  • Start your SOP first draft — submit to SOP Review for feedback
  • Attend all live sessions — outreach strategies & SOP workshop
  • Read Professor News Feed for latest faculty updates
Month 3 — September 2026: Drafting & Refinement
Refine your SOP, secure recommendation letters, and continue professor engagement.
  • Revise SOP based on mentor feedback (aim for 2–3 drafts)
  • Request recommendation letters — track in Rec Letters section
  • Follow up on professor outreach — update status in tracker
  • Upload writing samples / research papers to Files
  • Track your research papers in the Papers section
  • Start preparing for potential interviews
  • Attend live sessions — SOP review, interview prep
  • Early application deadlines start appearing — check University Tracker
Month 4 — October 2026: Applications Begin
Start submitting applications. Finalize all documents.
  • Finalize SOP — customize per university
  • Ensure all recommendation letters are confirmed/received
  • Submit applications to universities with early deadlines
  • Update application status in University Tracker (researching → applied)
  • Track interview invitations in the Interviews section
  • Practice mock interviews (use Prep Notes sub-tab)
  • Continue professor follow-ups
  • October — Many Fall 2027 early deadlines
Month 5 — November 2026: Final Push
Complete remaining applications and prepare for interviews.
  • Submit all remaining applications before December deadlines
  • Final SOP polish for December-deadline universities
  • Follow up on rec letter status — ensure all submitted
  • Prepare for interviews (live mock sessions)
  • Update all tracker statuses on dashboard
  • Review your Application Kanban for a complete overview
  • November–December — Most PhD application deadlines

3. Your Dashboard — Feature Guide

Everything you need is on your dashboard. Here's what each section does:

Overview
Your home base. See upcoming sessions, pending tasks, announcements, and quick stats at a glance.
PhD Openings
428 curated openings from US/CA/AU universities. Search by research area, university, or faculty. Save the ones you're interested in.
University Pool
Browse universities ranked by CS research output. Filter by country (US, Canada, Australia). Add to your personal tracker.
Professor Pool
Database of CS professors. Find potential advisors, check their research areas, and add them to your outreach list.
Research Labs
Labs actively hiring PhD students. See PI info, research areas, funding type, and hiring status.
My Universities
Your personal university shortlist. Track application status, deadlines, and days remaining for each.
My Professors
Track professor outreach. Record email status, response, and generate cold email templates.
Sessions
Live group sessions schedule. View past session recordings and attendance records.
My Tasks
Assignments from mentors with deadlines. Submit your work and receive grades with rubric-based feedback.
SOP Review
Submit your Statement of Purpose drafts. Mentors provide written feedback. Track versions over time.
My Files
Upload CV, SOP, writing samples, transcripts. Version history keeps track of all revisions.
Rec Letters
Track recommendation letter requests. Monitor status from requested to received for each university.
My Papers
Log your research papers, publications, and submissions. Track status from draft to published.
Interviews
Track interview invitations by university. Log prep notes, practice sessions, and outcomes.
App Kanban
Visual kanban board of your applications. Drag between stages: Researching → Applying → Applied → Interview → Decision.
Doc Checklist
9-item checklist of required documents. Track what's ready and what still needs work.
Calendar
Monthly view combining sessions, meetings, and university deadlines in one place.
Professor News
Latest updates from professors on X/Twitter. Stay informed about faculty moves and research updates.
Leaderboard
See how you compare with peers on tasks, attendance, and grades. Opt out if you prefer privacy.
My Payments
View your payment plan, submit payment proofs, and track payment status.
My Journal
Private journal for reflections, notes, and progress tracking. Only visible to you.
Book a Slot
Book 1:1 meetings with mentors during their available time slots.
Resources
Curated guides, templates, and reference materials shared by mentors and admin.
My Badges
Achievement badges earned automatically: Early Bird, Task Master, Paper Author, and more.
Global Search (Ctrl+K): Press Ctrl+K (or Cmd+K on Mac) anywhere on the dashboard to quickly search across professors, universities, labs, news, and more.

7. How to Get Help

  • Discord: Post questions in the relevant channel. Mentors and peers are active daily.
  • Book a 1:1: Use the "Book a Slot" feature on the dashboard to schedule time with a mentor.
  • Live Sessions: Bring your questions to the weekly sessions.
  • Dashboard Journal: Use your private journal to track thoughts, and share concerns during mentor check-ins.
  • Announcements: Check the dashboard Overview page regularly for important updates.

8. Tips for Success

  1. Start early. Don't wait until October to begin your SOP or professor outreach.
  2. Use the dashboard daily. Update your trackers, check tasks, and stay on top of deadlines.
  3. Apply broadly but strategically. 8–12 universities is the sweet spot. Mix reach, match, and safety schools.
  4. Personalize every email. Generic emails to professors get ignored. Reference their specific papers.
  5. Get feedback early and often. Submit SOP drafts as soon as possible. Iteration is key.
  6. Track everything. Every email sent, every deadline, every document — log it on the dashboard.
  7. Help your peers. Share useful resources on Discord. Teaching others reinforces your own understanding.
  8. Don't skip sessions. Attendance matters, and you'll miss critical tips and strategies.
