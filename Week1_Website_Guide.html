Week 1
Personal Academic Website — Step-by-Step Guide
1
Why You Need an Academic Website
  • Professors Google your name before replying to emails — control what they find.
  • A central hub for your CV, publications, research interests, and contact info.
  • Shows technical competence and professional branding — gives you an edge over other applicants.
  • Most PhD applicants from Bangladesh don't have one — you will stand out.
2
Choose Your Template
Template Tech Stack Difficulty Best For Demo

Recommendation: We recommend starting with Academic Pages or Minimal Academic Website. Both are free, easy to set up, and widely used by PhD students worldwide.
3
Option A — Academic Pages (Recommended)
1
Create a GitHub Account
  • Go to github.com and click "Sign up".
  • Choose a professional username (e.g., firstname-lastname or firstnamelastname).
  • Verify your email address.
Tip: Your username will be part of your website URL — choose carefully.
2
Fork the Template
  • Go to: github.com/academicpages/academicpages.github.io
  • Click the green "Use this template" button (top right) → "Create a new repository".
  • Name your repository exactly: yourusername.github.io (replace with your actual GitHub username).
  • Set visibility to "Public".
  • Click "Create repository".

Enable GitHub Pages
  • Go to your new repository's Settings tab.
  • In the left sidebar, click "Pages".
  • Under "Source", select "Deploy from a branch".
  • Branch: select main, folder: / (root).
  • Click "Save".
  • Wait 2–3 minutes, then visit https://yourusername.github.io.

Edit _config.yml (Site Settings)
  • In your repository, click on the file _config.yml.
  • Click the pencil icon (Edit this file) and update these fields:
title: "Your Full Name" name: "Your Full Name" description: "PhD Applicant | Computer Science | Your University" url: "https://yourusername.github.io" repository: "yourusername/yourusername.github.io"
  • Under author:, update:
name: "Your Full Name" bio: "PhD applicant interested in [your field]" location: "Dhaka, Bangladesh" employer: "Your University" email: "your.email@example.com" googlescholar: "https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=YOURID" orcid: "https://orcid.org/YOUR-ORCID" linkedin: "your-linkedin-username" github: "yourusername"
  • Click "Commit changes" at the bottom.

5
Update Your About Page
  • Navigate to _pages/about.md in your repository.
  • Click the pencil icon to edit.
  • Write your bio (2–3 paragraphs):
  • Paragraph 1: Who you are, current position, institution.
  • Paragraph 2: Your research interests and focus areas.
  • Paragraph 3: What you're looking for (PhD position, research opportunities).
# Example bio: I am a recent graduate from [University] with a [degree] in [field]. My research focuses on [topic], where I have worked on [brief description]. I am currently seeking PhD positions in [field] starting Fall 2027. My research interests include [interest 1], [interest 2], and [interest 3].
  • Commit changes.
6
Add Your Publications
  • Navigate to the _publications/ folder.
  • Click "Add file" → "Create new file".
  • Name it: 2024-01-01-your-paper-title.md
  • Use this format:
--- title: "Your Paper Title" collection: publications permalink: /publication/your-paper-title date: 2024-01-01 venue: 'Conference or Journal Name' paperurl: 'https://link-to-paper.com' citation: 'Your Name, Co-Author. (2024). "Paper Title." Conference Name.' --- Abstract or brief description of your paper goes here.
  • Repeat for each publication.
  • If no publications yet, skip this or add "in preparation" papers.

7
Upload Your CV
  • Navigate to the files/ folder in your repository.
  • Click "Add file" → "Upload files".
  • Drag your CV PDF into the upload area — name it something like CV_YourName.pdf.
  • Commit the upload.
  • Link it from your About page: [Download my CV](/files/CV_YourName.pdf)

8
Update Navigation
  • Edit _data/navigation.yml.
  • The default menu items work well for most users:
main: - title: "Publications" url: /publications/ - title: "Talks" url: /talks/ - title: "Teaching" url: /teaching/ - title: "CV" url: /cv/ - title: "Blog" url: /year-archive/
  • Remove sections you don't need (e.g., Talks, Blog if not using them).
  • Commit changes.
9
Add a Profile Photo
  • Navigate to the images/ folder.
  • Upload a professional headshot (JPEG or PNG, square aspect ratio recommended).
  • Update _config.yml → author: → avatar: to point to your image filename.
  • Commit changes.
10
Verify & Polish
  • Visit https://yourusername.github.io
  • Check all pages load correctly.
  • Test on mobile (resize browser or use your phone).
  • Verify all links work — CV download, publication links, social links.
  • Ask a friend to review.

Option B — Minimal Academic Website (Quickest Setup)
1
Fork the Repository
  • Go to: github.com/yuhui-zh15/Minimal-Academic-Website
  • Click "Fork" → name it yourusername.github.io.
  • Set to Public → Create fork.
2
Enable GitHub Pages
  • Settings → Pages → Deploy from branch → main → Save.
  • Wait 2–3 minutes for the site to build.
3
Edit index.html
  • Open index.html in your repo and click the pencil icon.
  • Replace all placeholder text in brackets with your info:
  • Your name, title, institution.
  • Bio/about paragraph.
  • Research interests.
  • Education history.
  • News items.
  • Commit changes.
4
Update Publications
  • Edit publications.json with your papers.
  • Each entry needs: title, authors, venue, year, links.
  • Follow the existing format already in the file.
5
Replace Profile Photo
  • Upload your photo to the images/ folder.
  • Replace profile.jpeg with your own photo (keep the same filename, or update index.html to point to your filename).
6
Verify
  • Visit your site and check everything looks right.
Total setup time: ~15–20 minutes

Option C — Hugo Academic CV (Advanced)
Quick Deploy (browser-only, ~60 seconds):
  • Go to hugoblox.com.
  • Click "Get Started" → Choose "Academic CV" template.
  • Connect your GitHub account.
  • The site deploys automatically to GitHub Pages or Netlify.
Local Setup (for advanced users):
npx hugoblox create site --template academic-cv cd my-site hugo server
Key Features:
  • Auto-imports publications from BibTeX files.
  • Supports Jupyter notebooks as blog posts.
  • Built-in LaTeX math rendering.
  • Presentation slides with reveal.js.
Best for: Researchers with many publications who want automatic BibTeX import.
Link: github.com/HugoBlox/hugo-theme-academic-cv

Option D — Luo's Academic Homepage
Setup:
  • Fork from the template repository at luost.me/academic-homepage/.
  • Edit _config.yml with your info.
  • Customize sections: About, Education, Honors, News, Publications.
  • Supports institution logos next to education entries.
  • Publication entries support thumbnails and code links.
Key Features:
  • Clean, minimal design.
  • Publication showcase with images.
  • News section organized by year.
  • LaTeX support for equations.
Best for: Researchers who want a clean, publication-focused homepage.

8
Troubleshooting
404 Error
Repository name must exactly match yourusername.github.io. Check spelling — no extra spaces.
Site not updating
GitHub Pages takes 2–5 minutes to rebuild. Check the Actions tab in your repository for build status.
Build failed
Check the Actions tab → click the failed workflow → read the error. Usually a YAML formatting issue — check indentation, colons, and quotes.
Images not loading
Check file paths are correct. Use relative paths like /images/photo.jpg.
Custom domain
Optional for later. Go to Settings → Pages → Custom domain. You'll need to configure DNS with your domain provider.
HTTPS not working
GitHub Pages provides free HTTPS. Make sure "Enforce HTTPS" is checked in Settings → Pages.
