Academic CV Prep — Part 1

Academic CV vs Resume

Resume

Length1 page
FocusJob-focused
HighlightsSkills & work experience
Used forIndustry / corporate applications
ResearchMinimal or none
PublicationsNot typically listed

Academic CV

Length2–3+ pages
FocusResearch-focused
HighlightsPublications, conferences, teaching
Used forPhD applications, academic jobs
ResearchCentral — detailed project descriptions
PublicationsFull list with citation details
For PhD applications, always use an Academic CV. A one-page resume signals you don't understand the academic hiring culture.

Academic CV Prep

Sections of a PhD-Style CV

  • 1
    Education — degrees, thesis titles, GPA if strong (3.5+ out of 4.0)
  • 2
    Research Experience — lab positions, RA roles, thesis work with bullet-point contributions
  • 3
    Publications & Conferences — journal papers, conference proceedings, even "in preparation"
  • 4
    Technical Skills — programming languages, tools, frameworks, methodologies
  • 5
    Awards, Fellowships & Grants — scholarships, prizes, funded research
  • 6
    Teaching & Mentoring — TA roles, tutoring, courses taught or assisted
  • 7
    Professional Service — peer reviewing, committee roles, workshop/event organisation
  • 8
    References — 2–3 academic referees with name, title, institution, and email

Academic CV Prep

What Makes a Strong CV

✓ DO

  • ✓ Tailor your CV to the target field and supervisor
  • ✓ Use reverse chronological order throughout
  • ✓ Include exact dates for every entry (month & year)
  • ✓ Quantify achievements wherever possible
  • ✓ Use consistent, clean formatting throughout
  • ✓ Include thesis and major project titles
  • ✓ Start bullets with action verbs: analyzed, developed, designed, implemented

✗ DON'T

  • ✗ Include photo, age, or marital status
  • ✗ List irrelevant work experience (retail, delivery, etc.)
  • ✗ Use fancy coloured templates — keep it clean
  • ✗ Leave unexplained gaps in your timeline
  • ✗ Forget to proofread — one typo can cost you
  • ✗ Exceed 3 pages as a student (even 2 is fine early on)
  • ✗ Use paragraph form — always use bullet points with action verbs

Activity

Peer Review Exercise

  1. Share your draft CV (or a section outline) in the session chat
    PDF, Google Doc link, or even a text paste — anything works
  2. Get paired with a partner by the facilitator
    You will review each other's CV simultaneously
  3. Review using this checklist:
    • → Are all key sections present (Education, Research, Skills, References)?
    • → Is the formatting consistent throughout?
    • → Is there irrelevant content that should be removed?
    • → What is the strongest section — why?
    • → What section needs the most work?
  4. Share written feedback — paste directly in the chat or as comments in the doc

Professors Google You
Before replying to your email, most supervisors will search your name. Control what they find — make it count.

Central Hub
One link for everything: your CV, publications, research interests, contact details, and project demos.
🚀
Shows Initiative
Demonstrates technical competence, attention to professional branding, and that you take your research identity seriously.
🏆
Stand Out
Most PhD applicants from Bangladesh don't have a website. You will. That is a real competitive edge.

Personal Website

What Goes on Your Website

Required Sections

  • ✓
    About / Bio — 2–3 paragraph professional summary of who you are and what you research
  • ✓
    Research Interests — current focus areas and future directions you want to pursue
  • ✓
    Publications — full list with links to PDFs and/or abstracts
  • ✓
    Education — degrees, institutions, thesis titles
  • ✓
    CV Download — direct link to your PDF academic CV
  • ✓
    Contact — email address, Google Scholar, ResearchGate, LinkedIn

Optional Extras

  • +
    Blog — write about papers you read, conference experiences, research updates
  • +
    Projects — showcase code repositories, demos, or research tools you've built
  • +
    Teaching — courses taught or TA'd, lecture materials if shareable
  • +
    News — recent publications, awards, conference acceptances
Start with required sections only. You can add extras after your PhD applications are submitted.

Hands-On — Academic Pages

Step-by-Step Setup

Getting Started

  1. Go to github.com/academicpages and click "Use this template"
    Creates a copy in your GitHub account
  2. Name your repo yourusername.github.io
    Must match your GitHub username exactly
  3. Enable Pages: Settings → Pages → main branch
    Site goes live in ~2 minutes
  4. Edit _config.yml — name, bio, email, social links
  5. Edit _pages/about.md — write your 2-3 paragraph bio

Adding Content

  1. Add publications in _publications/ as Markdown files
    One .md file per paper with title, venue, date, citation
  2. Upload CV PDF to files/ folder
    Link it from your About page: [Download CV](/files/cv.pdf)
  3. Add your photo to images/ folder
    Update avatar field in _config.yml
  4. Edit _data/navigation.yml — remove unused menu items
    Keep only: Publications, CV, Contact
  5. Test on mobile & verify all links work
    Share URL with a friend for feedback
Full guide with all 4 template options: See the Personal Academic Website — Step-by-Step Guide handout. It includes detailed instructions for Academic Pages, Minimal Academic Website, Hugo Academic CV, and Luo's Academic Homepage.
