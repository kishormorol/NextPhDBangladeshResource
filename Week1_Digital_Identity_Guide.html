Week 1
Academic Digital Identity — Step-by-Step Guide
1
Why Digital Identity Matters
  • When you email a professor, the FIRST thing they do is Google your name.
  • Your digital identity = what appears in those search results.
  • A complete identity (website + Scholar + ORCID + professional email) signals a serious researcher.
  • An incomplete or messy online presence (Facebook photos, no academic profiles) can cost you opportunities.

7
Quick Setup Checklist
  • Professional email created (firstname.lastname format)
  • Email signature set up with name, title, research area, and links
  • Google Scholar profile created and made public
  • At least one publication/article added to Scholar (or "in preparation")
  • Research interests added to Scholar profile
  • ORCID registered — 16-digit iD obtained
  • ORCID added to CV header, website, and email signature
  • ResearchGate profile created with institution and department
  • Publications uploaded to ResearchGate (with PDF if possible)
  • Same name used across ALL platforms
  • Same professional photo used everywhere
  • CV header updated with all profile links
  • Website contact page links to all profiles
  • "Googled yourself" and verified results look professional
  • All profile URLs submitted on NextPhD platform
8
Common Mistakes to Avoid
Different names on different platforms
"Fatima Rahman" on Scholar but "F. Rahman" on ORCID. Pick one format and use it everywhere — inconsistency looks unprofessional and breaks search engine recognition.
No institutional email
If you don't have one, use firstname.lastname@gmail.com. Never use nicknames, numbers, or underscores. A bad email format undermines your first impression before the professor reads a single word.
Private Scholar profile
Professors can't find you if your profile is set to private. Always make it public — go to Scholar Settings and toggle "Make my profile public" to ON.
Empty profiles
A Scholar profile with zero papers looks worse than no profile. Add at least your thesis or papers "in preparation." An empty shell signals you don't care about your research identity.
No photo
Profiles without photos look abandoned and get far fewer views. Use the same professional headshot across Scholar, ResearchGate, ORCID, LinkedIn, and your website.
Outdated information
If you've graduated or changed institutions, update ALL profiles. Stale data (old affiliation, old email) confuses professors and can make your outreach emails bounce or seem untrustworthy.
Facebook/Instagram as top Google results
If personal social media dominates your Google results, your academic profiles aren't visible enough. Create all the profiles above — they will push professional results to the top within a few weeks.
