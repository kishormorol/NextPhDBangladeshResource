## 📅 Session Plan (22 Weeks) — Tentative

All sessions are held on **Sundays at 9:00 PM BDT** via Google Meet.

### Month 1 — Research Foundation & Position Identification

| Week | Date | Topic | Description |
|------|------|-------|-------------|
| 01 | Jun 28, 2026 | **Academic CV & Personal Website Prep** | Build a professional academic CV highlighting education, research, publications, and skills. Set up a personal website or portfolio to showcase your research profile and make a strong first impression on supervisors. |
| 02 | Jul 05, 2026 | **Finding Supervisors & Research Groups** | Read faculty profiles and lab sites; check recent papers, grants, and student outcomes for active fit. Build your initial list of target supervisors. |
| 03 | Jul 12, 2026 | **Research Gap Identification** | Read recent papers efficiently; spot methodological, dataset, and application gaps; connect identified gaps to a viable PhD topic. |
| 04 | Jul 19, 2026 | **Research Idea Development** | Convert gaps into PhD-level ideas: title, problem statement, objectives, contributions, novelty, and feasibility. Develop three candidate ideas plus one backup. |

### Month 2 — SOP & Research Statement

| Week | Date | Topic | Description |
|------|------|-------|-------------|
| 05 | Jul 26, 2026 | **Statement of Purpose Structure** | Opening paragraph technique; connect academic journey, research experience, target program, and future goals; avoid generic and formulaic writing. |
| 06 | Aug 02, 2026 | **Research Statement Structure** | SOP vs. research statement — understand the difference; explain prior work, current expertise, future research direction, and potential impact clearly. |
| 07 | Aug 09, 2026 | **Personal Statement & Research Vision** | Motivation without exaggeration; articulate career goals; show leadership, resilience, teamwork, and independence through concrete examples. |
| 08 | Aug 16, 2026 | **Scholarship Writing** | Show academic merit and institutional fit; tailor one unified profile across multiple scholarship prompts (RTP, GRS, and others). |

### Month 3 — PhD Proposal & Publication Strategy

| Week | Date | Topic | Description |
|------|------|-------|-------------|
| 09 | Aug 23, 2026 | **Introduction & Problem Statement** | Background and motivation for your research; define the problem, research questions, and objectives with precision; show why it matters to the field. |
| 10 | Aug 30, 2026 | **Literature Review & Research Gap** | Organize literature by theme; compare methods, datasets, limitations; write a critical review, not a summary. Build a 30-paper literature matrix. |
| 11 | Sep 06, 2026 | **Methodology & Research Framework** | Design your method: data, preprocessing, model architecture, evaluation metrics, and validation plan; build a research framework diagram. |
| 12 | Sep 13, 2026 | **Contribution, Timeline & Risk Plan** | Define expected contributions; build a 3-year PhD Gantt chart; identify risks and backup plans; outline your publication strategy and target venues. |

### Month 4 — Professor Outreach & Interviews

| Week | Date | Topic | Description |
|------|------|-------|-------------|
| 13 | Sep 20, 2026 | **Professor Email Writing** | Concise, customized cold emails; how to attach CV, proposal, and transcript correctly; write short, medium, and detailed email versions for different scenarios. |
| 14 | Sep 27, 2026 | **Supervisor Meeting Preparation** | 2-minute self-introduction; how to discuss your ideas, funding situation, fit, and timeline professionally; run mock supervisor meetings with your mentor. |
| 15 | Oct 04, 2026 | **Research Presentation** | Structure a 10-minute research talk: problem, methodology, results, novelty, future work; learn to explain your research to non-specialists clearly. |
| 16 | Oct 11, 2026 | **Interview Question Bank** | Answer weakness, funding, and methodology questions confidently; avoid sounding memorized; complete a full mock interview with mentor feedback. |

### Month 5 — Submission & Final Review

| Week | Date | Topic | Description |
|------|------|-------|-------------|
| 17 | Oct 18, 2026 | **Portal & Document Management** | Complete application portals step by step; name and organize all documents; manage submission deadlines and recommender requests systematically. |
| 18 | Oct 25, 2026 | **Scholarship Applications** | Respond to scholarship prompts effectively; present your research excellence; align your proposal narrative with each university's stated priorities. |
| 19 | Nov 01, 2026 | **Final Document Review** | Proofread SOP, personal statement, and proposal; ensure consistency across all documents; replace vague claims with specific, measurable examples. |
| 20 | Nov 08, 2026 | **Final Mock Interview & Readiness Check** | Handle interview pressure and unexpected questions; finalize all application files; plan post-application follow-up and supervisor contact strategy. |
| 21 | Nov 15, 2026 | **Recommendation Follow-up & Deadline Tracking** | Follow up professionally with recommenders; confirm all letters are submitted; run a final deadline audit across all programs on your shortlist. |
| 22 | Nov 22, 2026 | **Final Submission Checklist & Program Wrap-up** | Complete submission readiness check for every application; confirm all portals are submitted; cohort wrap-up, next steps, and post-submission strategy. |
